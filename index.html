Renjie Zhu 朱仁杰

“detective” in the scientific world

I am a PhD student in Chemistry at Boston University, advised by Prof. Qiang Cui. I completed my B.S. in Chemistry at East China Normal University (2021–2025), where I worked with Prof. Fei Xia at the NYU-ECNU Center for Computational Chemistry on coarse-grained force fields and molecular simulations.

I think of molecular simulations as a detective's lens — a way to uncover the hidden dynamics of biological systems that cannot be seen by experiment alone. My current research focuses on coarse-grained molecular modeling, protein allosteric networks, and computer-aided drug design.

Prior to BU, I conducted a summer internship at Westlake University with Prof. Jing Huang on protein pocket alignment (2024), and spent four years developing CG force fields in Prof. Fei Xia's group at the NYU-ECNU Center (2021–2025).

Trivia: I love Pokémon, anime, fishing, and photography. I plan to collect more beautiful moments through my lens wherever fieldwork takes me.
